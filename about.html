WHO ARE WE?

John Michael C. Revagorda is a computer programmer hailing from Dumaguete City, Negros Oriental. Studying BS IT with Rianne Garcia, he enjoys toying with codes found on systems and learning how each line of code work. At his free time, he draws several artworks that span on digital artworks and traditional. As a self-taught artist who attended past art webinars, he utilizes his drawing tablet, his pencil and pen, and his keen art skills to draw different characters from different games. As of now, he is dealing with the balancing of his school life and his personal time.

Rianne Gracielle M. Garcia is a multimedia artist whose mediums include traditional and digital artworks. Despite her love for digital art, she is always interested in exploring different media and materials. She is now a first year taking Bachelor of Information Technology, studying at Silliman University. Originally from Cebu City and currently residing in Dumaguete City, Negros Oriental. Involved in animation and manga at a young age, Rianne became interested in art. Having transferred from Cebu to Bacolod six times in grade school to high school, she has been able to advance and grow by participating in theater workshops and orgs. A dreamer who never gives up

Bipolar



Sometimes the sun shines brightly, and you feel on top of the world, wind rushing through your hair. Other times, storm clouds gather, and you're plunged into a dark, tumultuous ride. Bipolar navigates these extreme highs and lows, where the emotional weather can change without warning making the ride of life both thrilling and challenging.

Anxiety



It's that knot in your stomach, a tightly wound ball of yarn that threatens to unravel at the slightest provocation. It's like navigating a maze with no map, where every turn feels like a potential dead end. a storm within, with thunderous thoughts and lightning flashes of worry. The mind becomes a turbulent sea, each wave carrying concerns that crash against the shores of your consciousness. It's like trying to hold onto a handful of sand while the grains slip through your fingers—one anxious thought at a time.

Paranoia



Imagine a delicate web spun in the recesses of the mind, where shadows morph into lurking doubts and whispers become conspiracies. Paranoia, a symphony of anxious whispers, is like a dense fog that distorts the clarity of thought, turning everyday interactions into a maze of hidden meanings and veiled threats. It's as if the mind wears tinted glasses, coloring reality with hues of suspicion and every gesture becomes a coded message waiting to be deciphered. In this intricate dance of uncertainty, the line between genuine concern and irrational fear blurs, leaving the mind caught in a perpetual tug-of-war between reason and the elusive specter of paranoia.

Blank



Standing in the middle of a crowded room, surrounded by a cacophony of voices, yet wearing soundproof headphones. The world is vibrant and alive around you, but you're encapsulated in a bubble of detachment. Colors lose their brilliance, and emotions become distant echoes. It's not a storm of sadness or a burst of joy; instead, it's the eerie calm of a stagnant pond, where ripples of feeling struggle to reach the surface.

Who Am I



A state where your mind puts up a barrier, making you feel disconnected from your thoughts, feelings, or surroundings. You see the scenes unfolding, but it feels distant and disconnected, as if the storyline doesn't quite belong to you. It's like being an audience to your own life, where the scenes lose their vibrancy and you become a passive spectator.
